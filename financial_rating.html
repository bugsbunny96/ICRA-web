PROCESS FOR FINANCIAL CREDIT RATING

1- What is Financial Credit Rating


Financial credit rating is an assessment of how likely a financial institution is to go out of business or default. Financial Institutions have their own credit ratings based on an estimate of how likely they are to default on their debts and go out of business. These credit ratings provide third-party insight into the reliability and risk level of financial institutions.

2- What is a Credit Rating Agency (CRA)


A credit rating agency is a company that assesses the financial strength of companies, financial institutions, government entities, especially their ability to meet principal and interest payments on their debts. The rating assigned to a given entity shows an agency’s level of confidence that the borrower will honor its debt obligations as agreed.

3- Core Parameters of Financial Credit Rating


Financial Credit Ratings involve assessment of asset quality, capitalization, earnings and resource risk profile as the core parameters that drive the credit risk profile of a bank /FI. The interplay of these parameters determines the ability to underwrite, price and manage risks, maintain adequate capital to absorb losses during times of stress, and ensure profitable growth. On a macro level, an analysis of economic and industry risk is also conducted.

4- Rating Grades and their meaning


ICRA will adopt the following rating scales while assigning final rating to financial institutions.

5- Steps involved in Credit Rating Process at ICRA LLC


a- Receiving formal request from Client
The credit rating process begins when the issuer (who wants to get a credit rating) issues the formal request for credit rating to ICRA LLC along with necessary documents. When ICRA accepts the request, an agreement is made between the issuer company and ICRA LLC and the customer will pay the required fee to ICRA.

b- Assigning to Credit Risk Team
In the second step, ICRA assigns the job and duties of credit rating to Credit Risk team who is responsible for rating projects/assignments and have expertise in the relevant business area.

c- Obtaining financial information
Credit Risk team will collect all the required information of the client relating to its financial statements and other relevant information from the given documents.

d- Research and financial statement analysis
In this step, Credit Risk team research client’s operations, validate KYC documents, analyze financial statements and cash flow projections. Credit risk team may communicate with the client for better understanding of the business model and future business prospects and may ask for additional documents and information wherever it deems necessary.

e- Discussion meeting
When all the analysis has been done, Credit Risk team will discuss the findings with the Board of Governors/Rating Committee, comprising senior management of ICRA LLC. All the issues having an effect on the company are identified. An opinion on the rating is also formed and the results of the team’s analysis are finally presented to the Board of Governors/Rating Committee.

f- Rating Committee meeting
This is the final authority meeting for assigning ratings and the work of the Credit Risk team comes to end. Now final authority will check facts, findings, and factors like political, social, and other factors. After considering all the results, facts, and other information the ICRA LLC will assign the actual Credit Rating.
